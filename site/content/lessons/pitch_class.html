Pitch Classes and Octave Numbering

When we talk about pitches by their letter names (B, G♯, E♭) we talking in terms of pitch class. In western music, we repeat the same 12 pitch classes in each octave and we consider repetitions of these pitch classes to be equivalent (see octave equivalence).

When it does not matter which C you are talking about, using pitch classes is useful. However, when it is important to know whether you are talking about a high C or a low C, pitch classes do not give us enough information.
